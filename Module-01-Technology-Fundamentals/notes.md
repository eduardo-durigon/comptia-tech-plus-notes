## Overview

Module 1 introduces the fundamental concepts behind how computers and digital systems work.  
It covers the **computing cycle**, the different **types of computing devices**, and the **core hardware components** that allow technology to process, store, and output information.

---

## The Computing Cycle

Every computer or digital device follows a basic operational process called the **computing cycle**.

**Four stages:**

1. **Input**
2. **Processing**
3. **Storage**
4. **Output**

This cycle happens **billions of times per second** in modern computers.

### Input

Input refers to the data or commands given to a computer.

Common input devices include:

- Keyboard
- Mouse
- Microphone
- Touchscreen
- Stylus / digital pen
- Trackball

Input quality matters — poor input leads to poor results.

Example concept:

> *Garbage In, Garbage Out (GIGO)* — incorrect input produces incorrect output.

---

### Processing

Processing is performed by the **CPU (Central Processing Unit)**.

The CPU:

- Interprets instructions
- Performs calculations
- Executes commands from software and the operating system

Processing happens extremely fast — often measured in **nanoseconds** (one billionth of a second).

---

### Memory (RAM)

**RAM (Random Access Memory)** is temporary working memory used by the CPU.

Characteristics:

- **Volatile memory** (data disappears when power is off)
- Stores active programs and data
- Allows fast access for processing tasks

The CPU loads information into RAM, processes it, and then may store results elsewhere.

---

### Storage

Storage keeps data **long-term**.

Types of storage include:

**Hard Disk Drives (HDD)**  
- Magnetic disks  
- Installed inside computers  
- Slower but larger capacity

**Solid State Drives (SSD)**  
- Use flash memory  
- Much faster than HDDs  
- No moving parts

**External Storage**

- USB flash drives
- External hard drives

---

### Optical Storage

Optical storage devices use lasers to read and write data.

Examples:

- **CD-ROM**
- **DVD**
- **Blu-ray**

Some optical disks are **read-only**, while others can be rewritten.

Examples:

- CD-RW
- DVD-R

---

### Output

Output is the result produced by the computer after processing.

Examples of output devices:

- Monitors
- Speakers
- Printers
- Headsets

Output may be displayed, printed, or stored for later use.

---

## Information Technology (IT)

**Information Technology (IT)** refers to the use of computers and systems to:

- Process data
- Store information
- Communicate digitally
- Manage technological infrastructure

IT systems rely on both **hardware and software** working together.

---

## Peripheral Devices

**Peripherals** are devices connected to a computer but not part of the core internal components.

Examples include:

- Printers
- Speakers
- External storage
- Monitors
- Keyboards
- Mice

These devices expand the computer’s capabilities.

---

## Types of Computing Devices

### Desktop Computers

Characteristics:

- Large and stationary
- Easier to repair and upgrade
- Usually more powerful for the price
- Require constant power

Common in offices and homes.

---

### Laptops / Notebooks

Characteristics:

- Portable
- Built-in battery
- Wireless connectivity
- More compact hardware

Limitations:

- Harder to repair
- Usually less powerful than desktops
- Often more expensive for similar performance

---

### Hybrid Laptops

Hybrid devices combine:

- Laptop functionality
- Tablet-style touchscreens

Examples include **2-in-1 convertible laptops**.

---

### Servers

Servers are powerful computers designed to:

- Store large amounts of data
- Host websites
- Manage email systems
- Handle many users simultaneously

Servers often include:

- Multiple CPUs
- Large memory capacity
- Redundant components for reliability

---

### Mobile Devices

Mobile devices include:

- Smartphones
- Tablets
- Handheld devices

Characteristics:

- Portable
- Specialized operating systems
- Limited hardware upgrades

---

### Wearable Devices

Wearables are small devices worn on the body.

Examples:

- Smartwatches
- Fitness trackers

These often connect to smartphones and collect health or activity data.

---

### Gaming Devices

Gaming systems focus on graphical performance.

Key component:

**GPU (Graphics Processing Unit)**

GPUs enable:

- High-performance graphics
- 3D rendering
- Ray tracing technology

Gaming systems are often connected to high-definition displays.

---

## Emerging Technologies

### Augmented Reality (AR)

Augmented Reality overlays digital elements onto the real world.

Example uses:

- Mobile apps
- AR glasses
- Industrial design tools

---

### Virtual Reality (VR)

Virtual Reality creates a fully digital environment.

Users interact with a simulated world using:

- VR headsets
- Motion sensors

Applications include:

- Gaming
- Training simulations
- Education

---

### Internet of Things (IoT)

The **Internet of Things (IoT)** refers to everyday devices connected to the internet.

These devices:

- Collect data
- Process information
- Communicate with other devices

Examples:

- Smart home systems
- Security cameras
- Smart thermostats
- Wearable health monitors

---

## Networking Basics

### DHCP

**Dynamic Host Configuration Protocol (DHCP)** automatically assigns IP addresses to devices when they join a network.

Benefits:

- Simplifies network management
- Prevents address conflicts
- Allows devices to connect automatically

---

## Key Takeaways

- All computers operate using the **input → processing → storage → output cycle**.
- **CPU, RAM, and storage** are essential hardware components.
- Many types of devices exist, including desktops, laptops, servers, and mobile devices.
- **Peripherals** expand computer functionality.
- Modern technology includes innovations such as **AR, VR, and IoT**.
- Networking services like **DHCP** automate device connectivity.
