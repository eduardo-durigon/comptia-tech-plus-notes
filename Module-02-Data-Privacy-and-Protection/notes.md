# Data and Personal Information

## What is Data?

Data refers to information collected, processed, and stored by computer systems.

Examples include:

- Names
- Addresses
- Financial records
- Online activity
- Health information

Organizations collect and analyze data to identify patterns and make decisions.

---

## Personally Identifiable Information (PII)

**PII (Personally Identifiable Information)** is any information that can identify an individual.

### Direct PII

Information that directly identifies a person.

Examples:

- Full name
- Passport number
- Bank account information
- Social security numbers

### Indirect PII

Information that may identify a person when combined with other data.

Examples:

- ZIP code
- Height or weight
- Race
- Religion
- Political views
- Demographic information

---

# Data Collection

## Algorithms

Algorithms are mathematical functions used to process and analyze data.

They often collect and analyze user information in the background.

Common uses include:

- Recommendation systems
- Advertising targeting
- Data analytics

---

## Cookies

Cookies are **small text files stored on a device** by websites.

They allow websites to:

- Remember user preferences
- Track browsing activity
- Personalize content

Different browsers store cookies separately.

---

## Data Brokers

**Data brokers** collect and sell user information to companies.

They gather information from:

- Social media
- Website activity
- Public records
- Online purchases

This information is often used for **advertising and analytics**.

---

# Privacy in Communications

## SMS Messaging

**SMS (Short Message Service)** is a texting system that uses cellular networks.

Characteristics:

- Sent over phone networks
- Not encrypted
- Stored as plain text
- Can potentially be intercepted

Because SMS is not secure, sensitive information should not be sent through it.

---

## Instant Messaging

Messaging apps such as:

- WhatsApp
- Signal

often use **end-to-end encryption**, making them more secure than SMS.

---

## Email Safety

Most email services do **not encrypt messages by default**.

Emails can:

- Be stored in plain text
- Be accessed by service providers
- Be read if a system is compromised

Encryption options exist but are not always used.

---

## File Sharing

**FTP (File Transfer Protocol)** allows file transfers over networks.

However:

- Traditional FTP does not encrypt files
- Data may be transmitted in plain text

Secure alternatives include encrypted file transfer systems.

---

# Encryption

## Encryption Basics

Encryption converts readable data (**plaintext**) into unreadable data (**ciphertext**).

A **key** is required to decrypt and read the information.

Encryption protects data from unauthorized access.

---

## Historical Encryption

### Caesar Cipher

One of the earliest encryption techniques.

It works by **shifting letters in the alphabet** by a fixed number of positions.

Example:

A shift of 3 turns:

A → D  
B → E  

---

## Modern Encryption

Modern encryption uses **complex mathematical algorithms**.

Encryption keys today are typically **128-bit or larger**.

The number of possible combinations becomes extremely large, making brute-force attacks difficult.

---

## Types of Encryption Keys

### Symmetric Encryption

- Uses **one key**
- Same key encrypts and decrypts data

### Asymmetric Encryption

- Uses **two keys**
- One encrypts
- One decrypts

---

## Data at Rest vs Data in Transit

### Data at Rest

Data stored on devices such as:

- Hard drives
- Databases
- Mobile devices

Protection methods include:

- Disk encryption
- Access permissions
- Lock screens

---

### Data in Transit

Data moving across networks.

Protected using secure protocols such as:

- **HTTPS**
- **TLS**

These protocols encrypt information while it travels across the internet.

---

# Virtual Private Networks (VPN)

A **VPN (Virtual Private Network)** creates an encrypted connection between devices and networks.

Benefits include:

- Protects internet traffic
- Hides user location
- Secures communication on public networks

VPNs create a **secure encrypted tunnel** for data transmission.

---

# Password Security

## Password Attacks

Common attacks include:

### Brute Force Attack

Trying many password combinations until one works.

### Dictionary Attack

Using lists of common words and passwords.

---

## Password Best Practices

Strong password rules include:

1. Do not share passwords
2. Do not reuse passwords
3. Avoid dictionary words
4. Avoid predictable patterns
5. Avoid personal information
6. Do not increment old passwords

---

## Password Managers

Password managers securely store passwords.

Examples:

- Google Password Manager
- Keeper
- Dashlane

Benefits:

- Generates strong passwords
- Stores credentials securely
- Requires only one master password

---

## Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

2FA adds an additional security layer.

Examples include:

- SMS verification codes
- Authentication apps
- Hardware security keys

Even if a password is stolen, access still requires the second factor.

---

# Data Breaches

A **data breach** occurs when sensitive information is exposed without authorization.

Example:

The **Target breach (2013)** exposed millions of credit card numbers.

Companies often offer **credit monitoring services** after breaches.

---

# Artificial Intelligence and Privacy

AI systems collect large amounts of data to train models.

Sources may include:

- Internet content
- Social media
- Licensed datasets
- Sometimes unlicensed sources

Privacy concerns include:

- Data ownership
- Consent
- Personal information usage

Users should always review privacy policies before sharing data with AI systems.

---

# Privacy Regulations

## GDPR

**General Data Protection Regulation (European Union)**

Key principles:

- Users must consent to data collection
- Companies must clearly explain how data is used
- Individuals can request data deletion
- Violations result in large fines

---

## CCPA

**California Consumer Privacy Act**

Gives California residents the right to:

- Know what data companies collect
- Request deletion of personal data
- Opt out of data selling

---

## HIPAA

**Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act**

Protects medical records and healthcare data.

Healthcare providers must keep patient information secure and private.

---

# Careers Related to Data and Privacy

## Data Privacy Attorney

Works with data protection laws and ensures organizations comply with privacy regulations.

Examples of regulations:

- GDPR
- CCPA

---

## Data Privacy Specialist

Focuses on privacy practices inside organizations.

Responsibilities include:

- Managing data policies
- Identifying privacy risks
- Ensuring compliance

---

## Data Analyst

Data analysts:

- Collect and organize data
- Identify patterns and trends
- Present insights using tools like:

Examples:

- Excel
- Tableau
- Power BI

---

# Key Takeaways

- Personal data must be protected to maintain privacy and security.
- Encryption converts readable data into protected ciphertext.
- Strong passwords and authentication methods reduce security risks.
- Privacy laws such as **GDPR, CCPA, and HIPAA** regulate data protection.
- Data is heavily used in modern systems including **AI and analytics**.
- Understanding data privacy is essential in cybersecurity.
